General Questions


Q.

Who are you people?

A.

Overworked, underpaid, disaffected but generally corporeal college students.


Q.

What is xchat-gnome?

A.

xchat-gnome is a frontend for the popular X-Chat IRC client targeted towards the GNOME platform. Our eventual goals include GNOME interoperability and HIG compliance.


Q.

Why is xchat-gnome?

A.

X-Chat has been a popular and successful open source IRC client for some time now. It is undebatably a stable, powerful and fully featured client, as proven by the number of faithful users it maintains. Unfortunately X-Chat's UI became cluttered over time as more features were gradually added. We view xchat-gnome as an opportunity to clean up the X-Chat user interface while (eventually ;)) keeping the stability and power that X-Chat users have come to expect.

Migrating from X-Chat gtk+

Q.

Can xchat-gnome be configured to use tabs?

A.

No. Adding this capability would require creating some very messy code, and doesn't really add anything to the program. xchat-gnome is not meant to replace X-Chat gtk+ for power users who are attached to its interface, but instead provide an alternative.

Q.

Why no tabs?

A.

The tree view started as an experiment. It turned out that it is significantly easier to scan visually than a long list of tabs. It also seems to have the side effect of removing any guilt that happens from being in a bazillion channels.

Q.

How is the server list edited?

A.

The server list is edited from the preferences window rather than the connect dialog.
